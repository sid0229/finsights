Understanding Credits & Loans

Credit and loans help individuals make significant purchases and manage financial gaps. However, responsible management is key to avoiding debt traps and maintaining a good credit score.

Types of Credit & Loans:

  • 1. Credit Cards: Short-term borrowing with interest, best used for convenience and building credit.
  • 2. Personal Loans: Unsecured loans with fixed interest rates, useful for consolidating debt or big purchases.
  • 3. Mortgages: Long-term loans for purchasing property, usually repaid over 15-30 years.
  • 4. Student Loans: Helps finance education, often with lower interest rates and flexible repayment options.
  • 5. Auto Loans: Used to finance vehicle purchases, typically secured by the car itself.
  • 6. Payday Loans: Short-term high-interest loans, generally not recommended due to high fees.
  • 7. Business Loans: Loans specifically for starting or expanding a business.
  • 8. Home Equity Loans: Borrowing against the value of your home for large expenses.
  • 9. Lines of Credit: Flexible borrowing options with a credit limit, often used by businesses.
  • 10. Microloans: Small loans, typically for entrepreneurs or individuals with limited access to traditional credit.
